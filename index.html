24-Week Training Plan Overview

With race day on August 23rd, a structured 24-week plan starts early March — which is now. Here's how to periodise your training across 6 phases to arrive race-ready.

Phase 1: Foundation & Open Water Introduction

Weeks 1-4 · Mar 3 – Mar 30

Build volume in all three disciplines. Priority: get comfortable in open water and start outdoor cycling as the weather improves.

  • Swim: 3× per week. Build to 2,000m continuous. Start open water sessions when possible (wetsuit essential — Scottish waters!)
  • Bike: 2-3× per week. Mix Zwift (intensity) with outdoor rides (bike handling, nutrition). Build to 60km
  • Run: 3× per week. 1 long run building to 14km, 1 easy run, 1 interval session. Keep easy runs at HR <130
  • Strength: Continue HIIT 1-2× per week, shifting focus to tri-specific movements (core, hip stability)
  • Weekly volume: ~8-10 hours

Phase 2: Endurance Building

Weeks 5-8 · Mar 31 – Apr 27

Push distances towards race requirements. Introduce brick sessions (bike→run back-to-back).

  • Swim: Build to 2,500m continuous. Practice sighting drills. Work on bilateral breathing
  • Bike: Build outdoor rides to 75km. Include 1 hilly ride per week for leg strength
  • Run: Long run to 16km. First brick session: 60min bike → 20min run (expect dead legs!)
  • Key session: Weekend brick — Saturday long bike, Sunday long run (or combined)
  • Weekly volume: ~10-12 hours

Phase 3: Race-Specific Training

Weeks 9-12 · Apr 28 – May 25

Hit race distances in training. Practice nutrition strategy. Open water should be regular now.

  • Swim: Regular open water sessions (fortnightly minimum). Build to race distance (1,930m) comfortably
  • Bike: First 90km ride. Practice eating/drinking on the bike — you'll need 60-90g carbs/hour on race day
  • Run: Long run to 18km. Brick sessions every 10-14 days: 90min bike → 30-40min run
  • Nutrition: Test race-day fuelling in training. Identify what your stomach tolerates at effort
  • Weekly volume: ~11-13 hours

Phase 4: Peak Training Block

Weeks 13-18 · May 26 – Jul 5

Highest training volume. This is where fitness is built. Continue VO₂ Max intervals to maintain top-end.

  • Swim: 3× per week including 1 open water. Refine pacing — don't go out too hard on race day
  • Bike: Regular 80-90km rides. Include race-pace efforts (hold NP ~170-180W for 2+ hours)
  • Run: Long run hits 20-21km. Maintain 1 speed session per week (VO₂ intervals or tempo)
  • Full brick rehearsal: Swim → Bike → short Run to practice all transitions
  • Weekly volume: ~12-14 hours (peak weeks)

Phase 5: Race Simulation & Sharpening

Weeks 19-22 · Jul 6 – Aug 2

Reduce volume slightly. Increase intensity of key sessions. Full race rehearsals.

  • Race rehearsal weekend: Full swim distance → 60-70km bike → 8-10km run. Test everything: kit, nutrition, pacing
  • Transition practice: Set up a mock transition area. Practice wetsuit removal, shoe changes, helmet on/off
  • Mental preparation: Visualise race day. Know the course. Plan your nutrition timing
  • Consider a sprint triathlon: Racing a shorter event in July would give invaluable race experience
  • Weekly volume: ~10-12 hours (controlled reduction)

Phase 6: Taper & Race Week

Weeks 23-24 · Aug 3 – Aug 23

Trust the training. Reduce volume by 40-50%. Keep a few short, sharp sessions. Prioritise sleep and nutrition.

  • Week -3: 70% normal volume. Include some race-pace efforts but keep them short
  • Week -2: 50% volume. Short swim, easy ride, easy run. Focus on sleep (your 8h+ target is crucial here)
  • Race week: Very light sessions Mon-Thu. 15min swim, 20min easy spin, 15min jog. Rest Fri-Sat
  • Pre-race: Carb-load Thu-Sat. Lay out all kit Saturday. Arrive early Sunday for a calm setup
  • Race morning: Light breakfast 3h before start. Arrive 90min early. Walk the transition area
Where You Stand vs. What You Need
Critical Focus Areas

🚨 Open Water Swimming

This is your biggest unknown. Pool swimming and open water are very different — sighting, drafting, cold water, no walls to push off. Start open water sessions as soon as possible (May onwards in Scotland). Consider a guided open water swim clinic. A wetsuit will help with buoyancy and warmth. This should be your #1 priority for the next 3 months.

⚠️ Brick Sessions (Bike→Run)

Running off the bike feels completely different to fresh running — your legs will feel like concrete for the first 1-2km. You must practice this regularly. Start with short bricks (45min bike → 15min run) and build. By July you should be doing 90min bike → 40min run bricks fortnightly.

⚠️ Race-Day Nutrition

A 70.3 lasting 5+ hours requires serious fuelling — you'll burn ~4,000-5,000 calories. Plan: light breakfast 3h before, gel/drink during swim exit, 60-90g carbs/hour on the bike (gels, bars, electrolyte drink), gels/cola on the run. Never try anything new on race day. Test everything in training first.

⚠️ Outdoor Cycling Distance

Your Zwift rides top out at 48km — race distance is 90km. Spring/summer is perfect for building outdoor distance on the roads around South Ayrshire. The Geordieman bike course is flat (Northumberland coast), which suits your profile, but you need time in the saddle outdoors to adapt to real-world conditions: wind, road surface, group dynamics.
